-SOUTHERN FIESTAS-


1. KERALA

-Onam is an annual Indian harvest and cultural festival related to Hinduism celebrated mostly by the people of Kerala. A major annual event for Keralites, it is the official festival of the state and includes a spectrum of cultural events

- In India's southern state of Kerala, Onam is considered the state festival. It commemorates New Year's Day and is also recognized as a harvest festival.

- According to Hindu mythology, Onam is observed in Kerala in honour of the wise leadership displayed by the mythical demon king Mahabali.Onam honours Vamana and King Mahabali.

2. TAMIL NADU

- Pongal also referred to as Thai Pongal is a multi-day Hindu harvest festival celebrated by Tamils in India and Sri Lanka. It is observed at the start of the month Thai according to Tamil solar calendar.

-According to tradition, the festival marks the end of winter solstice, and the start of the sun's six-month-long journey northwards when the sun enters the Capricorn, also called as Uttarayana.

- It is traditionally an occasion for decorating rice-powder based kolam artworks, offering prayers in the home, temples, getting together with family and friends, and exchanging gifts to renew social bonds of solidarity.

      Cultural Festival - PONGAL

3. KARNATAKA

- Ugadi festival marks the beginning of a new Hindu calendar year and is celebrated on the first day of “Chaitra” month, which falls in the months of March-April as per the Gregorian calendar.

- It is believed that Lord Brahma, the creator of the Universe created days, months, years and the vital elements of nature.

-It marks the beginning of a new year according to the Hindu calendar and is a time for new beginnings, fresh starts, and renewed energy.

-Ugadi pachadi is made of neem flowers, raw mango, jaggery, pepper powder, coconut and salt.

      Cultural Festival - UGADI

4. ANDRA PRADESH

- The festival Sankranti is celebrated for four days in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.Telugu women decorate the entrance of their homes by geometric patterns drawn using colored rice flour, called Muggu.

-During this time, the cultural supremacy of the state comes to the forefront as every place in Andhra Pradesh reflects the festive spirit.

-Time for celebrating the Makar Sankranti is usually in January, around the 14th of the month. It is celebrated in the month of Magh.

      Cultural Festival - MAKAR SANKRANTI

5. TELANGANA

- ‘Bathukamma' means ‘Mother Goddess come Alive’. Bathukamma is a beautiful flower stack, arranged with different unique seasonal flowers most of them with medicinal values, in seven concentric layers in the shape of temple gopuram

- Bathukamma, that means 'Mother Goddess come Alive' is celebrated towards the end of monsoon and heralds the abundance that nature has to offer. The festival begins on the day of Mahalaya Amavasya, and is celebrated with much fanfare for nine days.

- The main purpose of this particular festival is to pray with devotion to the Goddess in the strong belief that all young girls would get their beloved husbands as per their desire and wish soon.

      Cultural Festival - BATHUKAMMA
