East India is comprised of the states of West Bengal, Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Manipur, Nagaland, Mizoram, Tripura, and Orissa. This region is home to beaches and mountains and Cherrapunji, the city with the highest rainfall in the world.

- All tribes in East India have their own folk dances associated with their religion and festivals. The tribal heritage in the region is rich with the practice of hunting, land cultivation and indigenous crafts.

-Traditionally, Eastern cultures focused more on inner mastery and peace as the preconditions for a more peaceful world.



-EASTERN FIESTA-


1. WEST BENGAL

- Durga Puja also known as Durgotsava or Sharodotsav, is an annual Hindu festival originating in the Indian subcontinent which reveres and pays homage to the Hindu goddess Durga, and is also celebrated because of Durga's victory over Mahishasura.

- Durga Puja is a ten-day festival,of which the last five are of the most significance.The puja is performed in homes and public, the latter featuring a temporary stage and structural decorations

- It begins on the same day as Navratri, a nine-night festival in many northern and western states that more broadly celebrates the divine feminine (shakti).

      Cultural Festival - DURGA POOJA

2. ASSAM

-Bihu is a set of three important cultural festivals unique to the Indian state of Assam– 'Rongali' or 'Bohag Bihu' observed in April, 'Kongali' or 'Kati Bihu' observed in October or November, and 'Bhogali' or 'Magh Bihu' observed in January.

- The festival is a way of thanking the gods for a bountiful harvest and seeking their blessings for the upcoming year. Secondly, Rongali Bihu is a celebration of the rich cultural heritage of Assam.It marks the arrival of spring and the onset of the harvesting season

- People celebrate this day by singing traditional Bihu songs and dancing in an organized outdoor location, prayer halls, or fields.

      Cultural Festival - BIHU

3. MEGHALAYA

-Wangala is also called the festival of "The Hundred Drums", a harvest festival celebrated by the Garo tribe, who live in Meghalaya.

-In this post harvest festival, they give thanks to Misi Saljong the sun god, for blessing the people with a rich harvest. Wangala is celebrated in the months from September to December, with different villages setting different dates for the occasion.

- Wangala is traditionally celebrated for two to three days or up to a week by two or three collaborating villages.

-During Wangala, people young and old dress in their colourful garments and feathered headgear and dance to music played on long, oval-shaped drums.

      Cultural Festival - WANGALA

4. MANIPUR

-Yaosang is a festival celebrated in Manipur for five days in spring, starting on the full moon day of the month of Lamda. Yaosang is indigenous traditions of the Meitei people. It is considered the most important festival in Manipur. But unlike Holi, the celebrations go far beyond just colours.

-The Manipuris celebrate Holi as the Yaoshang festival. The festival is celebrated for five days. It is celebrated to mark the beginning of spring. Worshippers sing hymns and kirtans on this day

-Yaoshang, unlike Holi, is celebrated with a traditional twist in Manipur. During these five days Manipur comes alive with sporting events during the day and traditional “thabal chongba” dance in the night.

      Cultural festival - YAOSHANG

5. NAGALAND

-The Hornbill Festival is an annual festival celebrated from 1 to 10 of December in the Northeastern Indian state of Nagaland.The festival represents all ethnic groups of Nagaland for which it is also called the Festival of Festivals.

- The Festival is celebrated in Nagaland to encourage inter-tribal interaction and to preserve, protect and revive the uniqueness and richness of the Naga heritage. It is organized by State Tourism and Art & Culture Departments and also supported by Union Government.

-The festival is named after Hornbill, one of the most respected bird species in the state. Interestingly, the importance of the bird is reflected in several tribal cultural expressions, songs, and dances.

      Cultural Festival - HORNBILL

6. ARUNACHAL PRADESH

- Losar Festival is celebrated in the Ladakh region. It is celebrated to mark the beginning of the Ladakhi New Year. The festival is celebrated on the 1st day of the eleventh month of Tibetan Calendar.

- The Losar Festivals are celebrated in Arunachal Pradesh. It is celebrated by the people of the Monpa Tribe who are said to be inhabitants of Arunachal Pradesh.

-Losar is a 3-day festival, which is celebrated with great pomp and show in Tawang.The festival is observed to ward off evil spirits and welcome the the new year that shall be filled with happiness and prosperity.

      Cultural Festival - LOSAR
